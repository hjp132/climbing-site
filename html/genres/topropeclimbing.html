Top Rope Climbing

Top roping is a discipline in climbing which the climber is securely attached to a rope which is then passed through an anchor system, then down to a belayer at the bottom of the wall. The belayer takes in the slack rope throughout the climb to stop the climber from falling all the way to the bottom. Instead in the event of a fall the climer would then fall a short distance. Top rope climbing is commonly used in taller indoor climbing gyms and in other situations for example where it would be damaging the environment to drill / fix holes. An example of this would be sandstone rock is prone to erosion so it would be dangerous to rely on fixing bolts into it.
